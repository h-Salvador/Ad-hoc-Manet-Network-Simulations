A Performance Comparison of Wireless Ad Hoc Network Routing Protocols under Security Attack

Introduction

Welcome to the Wireless Ad Hoc Network Performance Comparison website! This project aims to evaluate the performance of Ad-hoc On-demand Distance Vector (AODV), Dynamic Source Routing (DSR), and Optimized Link State Routing (OLSR) protocols under security attacks in wireless ad hoc networks. Wireless ad hoc networks have gained significant popularity in various applications, including military operations, disaster management, healthcare, and remote sensing, among others. These networks provide flexibility and rapid deployment in scenarios where traditional infrastructure-based networks may not be available or reliable. However, the decentralized and dynamic nature of wireless ad hoc networks exposes them to security threats such as jamming, eavesdropping, and denial-of-service attacks. Addressing these security concerns is crucial to ensure the reliable and secure operation of wireless ad hoc networks. The evaluation and selection of appropriate routing protocols play a vital role in mitigating security risks and enabling efficient communication. By assessing the performance of AODV, DSR, and OLSR routing protocols under different security attack scenarios, this project aims to provide insights into their strengths and weaknesses. The findings will assist network engineers, researchers, and professionals in making informed decisions regarding protocol selection and contribute to the design and implementation of more secure and resilient wireless ad hoc networks.

Project Objectives

The objectives of this project are as follows:

  1. Evaluate the performance of Ad-hoc On-demand Distance Vector (AODV), Dynamic Source Routing (DSR), and Optimized Link State Routing (OLSR) protocols under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks in wireless ad hoc networks.
  2. Compare the performance of the selected routing protocols under different grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attack scenarios to analyze their effectiveness in the presence of these specific attacks.
  3. Identify the strengths and weaknesses of each routing protocol under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attack scenarios and assess their impact on key performance metrics such as packet delivery ratio, end-to-end delay, and throughput.
  4. Determine the most suitable routing protocol for specific grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attack scenarios based on the performance evaluations, considering the trade-off between security and network performance.
  5. Contribute to the design and implementation of more secure and reliable wireless ad hoc networks by providing insights into the performance and vulnerabilities of routing protocols under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks.
  6. Offer guidance and recommendations for network engineers, researchers, and professionals in selecting appropriate routing protocols and implementing effective countermeasures against grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks in wireless ad hoc networks.
  7. Enhance the understanding of the impact of grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks on the performance of wireless ad hoc network routing protocols and analyze their implications for network security.
  8. Generate comprehensive performance metrics, including packet delivery ratio, end-to-end delay, and throughput, tailored to assess the performance of routing protocols under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks.
  9. Create an intuitive and informative website that presents the results of the performance comparison under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks, providing interactive visualizations and insights for researchers and professionals in the field.
  10. Contribute to the advancement of knowledge in the field of wireless ad hoc network security by expanding the understanding of vulnerabilities and countermeasures associated with grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks in the context of routing protocols.

Methodology

This project employs a systematic methodology to evaluate the performance of Ad-hoc On-demand Distance Vector (AODV), Dynamic Source Routing (DSR), and Optimized Link State Routing (OLSR) protocols under grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks in wireless ad hoc networks. The methodology consists of the following key steps:

  1. Research: Conduct a comprehensive review of existing literature and research on wireless ad hoc network routing protocols, security attacks, and performance evaluation methodologies. This step ensures a solid understanding of the protocols, attacks, and evaluation metrics employed in the project.
  2. Simulation Setup: Design and configure a simulation environment using appropriate tools and frameworks. The simulation setup should accurately model the characteristics of wireless ad hoc networks and incorporate the specific grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attack scenarios to be evaluated.
  3. Protocol Implementation: Implement the AODV, DSR, and OLSR routing protocols within the simulation environment, configuring them to match real-world deployment settings. This includes defining protocol parameters, network topologies, and integration of security mechanisms.
  4. Attack Scenario Implementation: Integrate the grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attack scenarios into the simulation environment. This involves implementing the attack models and configuring their parameters, such as attack duration, intensity, and location within the network.
  5. Performance Evaluation: Design and execute a series of simulation experiments to collect performance metrics for each routing protocol under the various attack scenarios. Performance metrics may include packet delivery ratio, end-to-end delay, throughput, and other relevant network performance indicators.
  6. Data Analysis: Analyze the collected data using statistical techniques and visualization tools to compare the performance of the routing protocols. Evaluate the impact of grayhole, wormhole, and blackhole attacks on each protocol's performance and identify any significant differences or vulnerabilities.
  7. Website Development: Create a user-friendly website to present the results of the performance comparison. The website should include interactive visualizations, clear explanations of the findings, and recommendations based on the evaluation outcomes.
